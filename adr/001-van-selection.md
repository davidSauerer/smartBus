# ADR-001: Vehicle Selection for Camper Conversion

**Status:** Open
**Date:** 2026-04-09

---

## Context

### Use Case 1 — Work Commute (Berlin ↔ Frankfurt)
Monthly commute after work, ~550 km one way. The vehicle serves as a mobile home in Frankfurt for 2 to 5 days per stay. This happens year-round including winter. Primarily staying at campsites, occasionally on wild or urban spots.

When parked at home base in Berlin, the vehicle will use street parking or an open-air rental spot — no underground or covered parking required, so vehicle height is not a constraint. Note: some Berlin districts require a special parking permit for overnight stays of visibly converted or oversized vehicles (>6m length); the applicable rules should be checked for the specific district.

### Use Case 2 — European Vacations (with girlfriend)
Leisure travel across Europe, spring through autumn (no winter use for this case). Relaxed pace — max ~3 hours of driving per day. Two people sleeping and living in the van. Mainly campsites, but off-grid stays also planned.

### Use Case 3 — Mobile Office while Visiting Friends across Germany
Visiting friends in different parts of Germany for 1 to 3 days, with the van as a fallback workspace when needed. Full remote workdays (~8 hours), with frequent online meetings requiring active speaking. The van needs to be a viable place to work from anywhere.

### Use Case 4 — Daily Driver in Berlin
Occasional short trips around Berlin — supermarket runs, hardware store (Baumarkt), errands. This happens only 1–2 times per month; the van is not a primary daily car but needs to be usable in that role without major friction.

### Key Implications

Key implications for the vehicle choice:
- Comfortable for long highway drives (use case 1) and short urban driving (use cases 2, 4)
- Must physically accommodate 2 people sleeping and living (dimensions, fixed high roof)
- Year-round reliability required — diesel, auto, Euro 6, low mileage, recent registration
- Must be manageable in urban traffic and fit in standard street parking spots

## Interior Build Concept

This section describes the planned interior layout. It directly influences the minimum vehicle dimensions and is used to validate whether a specific platform/configuration actually fits the build.

### Sleeping & Living Area

**Requirements (what matters for vehicle selection):**

| Dimension | Requirement | Note |
|-----------|-------------|------|
| Bed length | 200 cm (must have) | Minimum for Person 1 at 186 cm — no reduction possible |
| Bed width | ~140 cm (should have) | 120 cm is the fallback minimum |

The exact build mechanism — sofa, seats, fold-out platform, or any other convertible solution — is not decided at this stage and does not affect the vehicle choice. What matters for selecting the vehicle is the floor area that must be available when the bed is deployed: **200 cm × 140 cm**.

**Interior width implication:** In bed mode the build needs 140 cm of interior width. All large panel van platforms have interior widths of ~183–215 cm above wheel arch height — 140 cm fits on every platform, leaving 43–75 cm on the other side for access.

### Kitchen

A minimal fixed kitchen unit with one portable element:

- **Sink** — fixed, with fresh and grey water tanks
- **Fridge** — compressor fridge (under-counter or built-in), sized for 2 people
- **Prep / counter space** — sufficient surface to prepare food
- **Cooktop** — portable 1-burner, not fixed; stored when not in use, placed on counter when cooking
- Under-counter storage for cookware and supplies

The portable cooktop means no permanent hob cut-out is needed in the counter, slightly reducing the fixed footprint.

Approximate footprint of fixed elements: **~75–80 cm length × ~55 cm depth** along one side wall.

### Floor Plan Requirement

Bed and kitchen placed sequentially along one wall sets the minimum usable interior length:

| Element | Length |
|---------|--------|
| Bed area (deployed) | 200 cm |
| Kitchen unit (fixed elements) | ~80 cm |
| **Minimum interior length** | **~280 cm** |

The build concept requires **≥ 280 cm** of usable interior length in practice — below the 250 cm must-have in the requirements table but tighter than the raw number suggests. Not all L2 configurations reach this — see the per-platform comparison in Step 2.

| Platform | L2 Interior Length | Fits build (≥280 cm)? |
|----------|-------------------|----------------------|
| Fiat Ducato / Peugeot Boxer L2 | ~302 cm | ✅ yes — 22 cm margin |
| Mercedes Sprinter L2 | ~293 cm | ✅ yes — 13 cm margin |
| VW Crafter L2 | ~291 cm | ✅ yes — 11 cm margin |
| Iveco Daily L2 | ~288 cm | ✅ yes — 8 cm margin |
| Ford Transit L2 | ~261 cm | ❌ no — 19 cm short; needs L3 |

**Key implication:** With the smaller kitchen footprint, all platforms except Ford Transit now fit the build comfortably in L2. The Ford Transit still requires L3 (~5.5 m overall), making it the only platform that conflicts with the length preference.

## Options Considered

### Step 1 — Vehicle Class Eligibility

All vehicle classes were evaluated against the must-have requirements before considering specific models.
Interior dimensions (length, height) vary slightly between manufacturers within the same L/H designation — figures below are representative ranges.

---

#### Class A: Large Panel Vans — GVW ≤ 3.5 t ✅ IN

Platforms: Fiat Ducato / Peugeot Boxer / Citroën Jumper · Renault Master / Opel Movano · Ford Transit · Mercedes Sprinter · VW Crafter · Iveco Daily

| Config | Int. Length | Int. Height | Length ≥250cm | Build fit ≥290cm | Seated ≥130cm | Overall length | Verdict |
|--------|-------------|-------------|---------------|-----------------|---------------|----------------|---------|
| L1H1   | ~243–250 cm | ~127–140 cm | ❌            | ❌              | ❌ head hits ceiling | ~4.9–5.0 m | ❌ OUT |
| L1H2   | ~243–250 cm | ~165–179 cm | ❌            | ❌              | ✅            | ~5.0–5.1 m    | ❌ OUT |
| L2H2   | ~261–302 cm | ~165–179 cm | ✅            | ⚠️ platform-dependent (see Step 2) | ✅ seated OK, no standing | ~5.2–5.4 m | ⚠️ MARGINAL — seated work fine but Person 1 cannot stand upright |
| L3H2   | ~320–370 cm | ~165–179 cm | ✅            | ✅              | ✅ seated OK, no standing | ~5.9–6.0 m | ❌ OUT — no standing + length penalty; L2H3 is strictly better |
| L2H3   | ~261–302 cm | ~190–217 cm | ✅            | ⚠️ platform-dependent (see Step 2) | ✅ seated ✅ standing ✅ | ~5.2–5.4 m | ✅ IN — preferred |
| L3H3   | ~320–370 cm | ~190–217 cm | ✅            | ✅              | ✅ seated ✅ standing ✅ | ~5.9–6.0 m | ⚠️ MARGINAL — only if no L2H3 platform fits the build |

**H1 definitively eliminated:** Interior height 127–140 cm — Person 1's head (seated) sits at ~144 cm, which exceeds H1 clearance on all platforms. Not viable for seated work.

**H2 demoted to marginal:** Interior height 165–179 cm clears the seated work requirement but forces Person 1 to crouch for all standing activities (cooking, changing, moving). L2H3 is the better choice at the same overall vehicle length — there is no reason to choose L2H2 over L2H3 for this build.

**L1H1 / L1H2 excluded:** Interior length at or below 250 cm on all platforms (Ducato/Boxer ~248 cm, Sprinter/Crafter ~243 cm). The sofa/bed unit alone requires 200 cm, leaving ≤50 cm for the kitchen — not viable regardless of roof height.

**L2 is not uniform:** Interior length in L2 ranges from 261 cm (Ford Transit) to 302 cm (Ducato/Boxer). Only platforms with ≥290 cm in L2 can accommodate the build without going to L3. See Step 2 for the per-platform breakdown.

**Preferred configurations: L2H3** — meets the build requirement on the right platforms, stays within 5.4 m overall, delivers standing height nice-to-have. L2H2 acceptable if standing height is deprioritised.

---

#### Class B: Compact / Medium Panel Vans ❌ OUT

Platforms: VW Transporter T6 / T6.1 · Mercedes Vito · Ford Transit Custom · Renault Trafic · Peugeot Expert / Citroën Dispatch

Interior cargo length across all variants (L1–L2, H1–H2): **~175–225 cm** — below the 250 cm must-have on every configuration. Eliminated regardless of roof height.

---

#### Class C: Small Delivery Vans ❌ OUT

Platforms: VW Caddy · Renault Kangoo · Opel Combo · Mercedes Citan · Ford Transit Connect

Interior length ~120–170 cm, payload ~500–750 kg. Fails interior length and payload requirements. Not in scope.

---

#### Class D: Large Minibuses ❌ OUT

Platforms: Mercedes Sprinter Tourer · VW Crafter Bus · Ford Transit Bus · Iveco Daily Combi

Based on the same platforms as Class A but come factory-glazed throughout. The "no side/rear cargo windows" should-have exists precisely to allow custom window placement during conversion — full factory glazing makes insulation and layout work significantly more complex. Eliminated unless sourced as a blank panel variant (which is just a Class A panel van).

---

#### Class E: Chassis-Cab with Box Body ❌ OUT (for this build)

Same drivetrains as Class A panel vans but require a separately fitted box body. Adds €5–15k in build cost and substantial complexity with no meaningful space advantage over a Class A L2H3. Not justified given the panel van market.

---

#### Class F: Box Trucks / Vehicles over 3.5 t GVW ❌ OUT

Exceed the 6 m length should-have, require a Category C driving licence above 3.5 t GVW, and are impractical for urban Berlin use. Eliminated.

---

#### Class G: SUVs · Pickups · People Carriers · Passenger Cars ❌ OUT

Interior cargo length well below 250 cm in all cases. Pickups have no enclosed weather-tight cargo area. People carriers (V-Class, Multivan) are passenger-optimised platforms with insufficient payload and interior volume for a full camper conversion. Eliminated.

### Step 2 — Specific Platform Comparison (Class A, L2 configurations)

All Class A platforms evaluated at L2 wheelbase — the preferred length. Figures are for the cargo/panel van variant (not minibus). Interior dimensions are manufacturer specs for the standard high-roof (H2/H3) variant.

| Platform | L2 Int. Length | Build fit (≥280 cm) | Overall length (L2) | Highway feel | Price range (2019+, ≤50k km) | Verdict |
|----------|---------------|---------------------|---------------------|--------------|------------------------------|---------|
| Fiat Ducato / Peugeot Boxer / Citroën Jumper | ~302 cm | ✅ +22 cm margin | ~5.41 m | ✅ car-derived platform, good highway manners | ✅ €18–25k, strong market availability | ✅ **Best fit** |
| Mercedes Sprinter | ~293 cm | ✅ +13 cm margin | ~5.25 m | ⚠️ more truck-like cab, heavier steering at speed | ❌ typically €28–40k for 2019+ ≤50k km — exceeds budget | ❌ OUT (price) |
| VW Crafter | ~291 cm | ✅ +11 cm margin | ~5.25 m | ✅ better than Sprinter; MQB-influenced feel | ⚠️ €24–32k — borderline to over budget | ⚠️ MARGINAL (price) |
| Renault Master / Opel Movano | ~298 cm | ✅ +18 cm margin | ~5.55 m | ⚠️ average; acceptable but not car-like | ✅ €18–24k | ⚠️ MARGINAL (length at 5.55 m, borderline on driveability) |
| Ford Transit | ~261 cm | ❌ −19 cm — needs L3 | L3: ~5.53 m | ✅✅ best highway feel in class, car-like | ✅ €20–26k | ⚠️ MARGINAL — excellent to drive but forced into L3, pushing length to edge of 5.5 m preference |
| Iveco Daily | ~288 cm | ✅ +8 cm margin | ~5.40 m | ❌ most truck-like character in this class | ⚠️ €20–26k, higher maintenance costs | ❌ OUT (driveability) |

**Summary:**

- **Fiat Ducato / Peugeot Boxer / Citroën Jumper (L2H3):** Only platform that clearly satisfies all must-haves simultaneously — build fit, length, price, and acceptable highway feel. Three badge variants of the same Sevel platform; choose based on dealer availability and price.
- **Ford Transit (L3H3):** Best highway driving feel in the class but must go to L3 to fit the build, landing at ~5.53 m overall. Still worth considering as a trade-off if driving character is weighted heavily.
- **VW Crafter (L2H3):** Technically fits but with only 1 cm margin on interior length — any aftermarket flooring or wall lining eats into that. Price likely above budget.
- **Sprinter and Iveco Daily:** Eliminated by price and driveability respectively.
